History of the Internet


The First Internet

When the USSR launched the artificial earth satellite in 1957, it sparked the interest of the US to form the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) within the Department of Defense (DOD) to establish US lead and protection in science and technology. The release of Sputnik 1 played an important role in developing global telecommunications or satellites have an important role in transmitting various forms of data at present.

The earliest ideas for a computer network intended to allow general communications among computer users were formulated by computer scientist J. C. R. Licklider of Bolt, Beranek and Newman (BBN) in 1962. In the same year, packet switching networks were developed in the year. It originated from the military because they need high security in transferring information of networks. Through packet-switching, the data is split into tiny packet that make different routes to a destination. Because of this technology, it makes it difficult for outsiders to eavesdrop on conversation. Moreover, networks ca withstand large scale destruction such as the feared nuclear attack during the cold war.

In 1969, ARPANET was commissioned by DoD for further research into networking. This was supposed to plan a larger scale for computer networks and accelerate knowledge transfer as well as avoid duplication of research. Along with ARPANET are other agencies such as the RAND Corporation USA (military corporation), National Physical Laboratory (commercial network in England) and CYCLADES (scientific network in France). These for were the foundation of the modern internet.
